## How God‚Äôs will became market forces

The transformation from religious to technocratic justification for exploitation represents one
of the most successful rhetorical shifts in modern history. Both systems serve identical
functions‚Äîlegitimizing wealth extraction from many to benefit few‚Äîbut technocratic
language presents itself as inevitable, scientific, neutral, and beyond moral contestation.

Slavery in America was justified through biblical interpretation: the curse of Ham, natural
racial hierarchy ordained by God, divine mandate for subjugation. Chinese Exclusion was
protecting ‚ÄúAmerican civilization.‚Äù Convict leasing after 1865 continued slavery under the
13th Amendment‚Äôs exception clause: ‚Äúexcept as punishment for crime.‚Äù Black Codes
criminalized Black life specifically to create a captive labor force. The language was explicit
about racial hierarchy and divine will.

By the early 20th century, ‚Äúscientific racism‚Äù and Social Darwinism began replacing religious
justifications. Herbert Spencer‚Äôs ‚Äúsurvival of the fittest‚Äù made inequality appear natural and
evolutionary. Eugenics provided pseudoscientific frameworks for racial hierarchy. Frederick
Winslow Taylor‚Äôs ‚Äúscientific management‚Äù techniques‚Äîstudying worker movements to
maximize efficiency and minimize autonomy‚Äîoriginated on slave plantations and were
repackaged as neutral optimization.

Auguste Comte articulated the ‚ÄúLaw of Three Stages‚Äù in which societies evolve from
theological to metaphysical to positive (scientific) reasoning. The Technocracy Movement of
the 1930s proposed replacing politicians with engineers managing the economy based on
energy production and consumption. After World War II, ‚Äúmodernization theory‚Äù and
development economics replaced missionary justifications for Western intervention in the
Global South. The language shifted from saving souls to maximizing GDP growth.

Contemporary justifications complete this evolution into what theologian William Stringfellow
called a system where ideology becomes unnecessary. The ‚Äúefficient, monumental swirling
together of technology, business, and government‚Äù controls through structure rather than
requiring explicit doctrine. Pope Francis in Laudato Si‚Äô identified the ‚Äútechnocratic paradigm‚Äù
in which all problems become technical questions solvable through expertise, making it
impossible to even question whether technical optimization should be the primary value.
Philosopher Augusto Del Noce argued that the distinctive ideology of technological society is
scientism, leading to ‚Äútechnocratic totalitarianism‚Äù that presents itself as value-neutral.

The parallel structures reveal the function beneath the language:

**Slavery era:** ‚ÄúGod ordained this hierarchy‚Äù ‚Üí Serves plantation owners‚Äô wealth extraction
